A Little About Myself

Ever since I was a child, I've been fascinated by computers. That a series of electrons flowing in specific patterns in controlled intervals could translate to everything from exploring a virtual alien world to accessing literally all of the information known to humanity was nothing short of miraculous to me. As I learned more about computers, programming, and how the development process worked, it only fed my drive to become a part of the world that made these miracles happen.

As a self-taught developer I've worked to learn as much as possible about front end website development, from simple HTML and CSS to more in-depth processes like JavaScript, jQuery, Sassy CSS, Bootstrap, and SVG design and animation. Every day I push myself to take on a new challenge, learn a new skill, or improve the skills I have.

Despite not completing a formal degree, I have taught myself the fundamentals of front-end web design via books, online courses, and other miscelaneous resources. Below I have listed my skill-sets as well as notable courses and projects completed. My coding work, including this CV site, has been completed via SublimeText3 as well as GitHub (preferrably via the bash terminal).

Languages and Skills

  • CSS3
  • JavaScript
  • jQuery
  • Modern/Mobile-friendly web design
  • Strong ability to find design docs and common fixes when needed
  • Quick learning of new languages, syntax, and programming/design concepts

Planned Learning

  • Angular JS

I would love nothing more than to go back to school however life, as it is wont to do, has prevented me from finding both the funds and the free time to pursue this. Never one to let a goal die, however, I have utilized the amazing array of free and affordable online resources to learn the languages and nuances of the net. I have worked with small businesses, friends, and family to seek new projects and challenges to better learn web design from a practical and realistic standpoint.

This has, in my opinion, allowed me several advantages as a burgeoning web developer in the form of a lack of company specific workflow blinders and habits. I am a blank slate, more than willing to learn and adapt to a potential employer's workflow and culture. I have no preconceptions of how the job "should" be done, allowing myself to quickly learn the preferences of the team I work with.
